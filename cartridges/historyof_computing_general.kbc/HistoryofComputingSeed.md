# **History of Computing**

## **Theoretical Foundations**

* Alan Turing's "Universal Machine" concept proved that a single machine could perform any computable task | Foundations | 0.98  
* The Von Neumann Architecture describes a computer design where program data and instruction data share the same memory | Architecture | 0.97  
* Ada Lovelace is credited as the first computer programmer for her work on Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine | Pioneers | 0.96  
* Claude Shannon's thesis proved that Boolean algebra could be used to simplify the design of telephone switching circuits | Foundations | 0.97  
* The Church-Turing Thesis states that anything that can be computed by an algorithm can be computed by a Turing machine | Foundations | 0.95

## **Evolution of Systems**

* The ENIAC was the first programmable, electronic, general-purpose digital computer | Milestones | 0.96  
* The Transistor replaced vacuum tubes, leading to the second generation of computers and massive miniaturization | Hardware | 0.98  
* LISP (List Processing) was created by John McCarthy in 1958 and became the foundational language for early AI | Software | 0.94  
* The Memex, proposed by Vannevar Bush in 1945, was a theoretical proto-hypertext system for storing and linking knowledge | Concepts | 0.93  
* The Xerox PARC "Alto" introduced the Graphical User Interface (GUI), the mouse, and bitmapped displays | Milestones | 0.95

## **AI and Logic History**

* The Dartmouth Workshop (1956) is considered the founding event of Artificial Intelligence as a field | AI History | 0.94  
* Connectionism is an approach to AI that models mental or behavioral phenomena as the emergent processes of interconnected networks | AI Theory | 0.92  
* The "AI Winter" refers to periods of reduced funding and interest in AI research following over-hyped expectations | AI History | 0.95  
* Symbolic AI (Good Old Fashioned AI) relies on high-level, human-readable representations of problems and logic | AI Theory | 0.93
