# **Cybernetics**

## **Control Systems and Feedback**

* A Feedback Loop is a process where the output of a system is circled back as input, a core L1 axiom for Kitbash stability | Feedback | 0.98  
* Negative Feedback seeks to maintain a set point by counteracting deviations, the basis of Homeostatic Loops | Control Theory | 0.97  
* Positive Feedback amplifies deviations from a set point, potentially leading to system "Runaway" or Phase Transitions | Control Theory | 0.95  
* Feedforward control anticipates disturbances before they affect the system, an L3 heuristic for proactive regulation | Control Theory | 0.93  
* The Governor (Watt's Governor) is a classic L0 physical implementation of mechanical feedback | History | 0.96

## **Information and Communication in Systems**

* Variety (Ashby’s Law of Requisite Variety) states that only variety can destroy variety; a controller must have at least as many states as the system it controls | System Laws | 0.96  
* Circular Causality is the principle that in complex systems, cause and effect are mutually influential | Logic | 0.94  
* Black Box theory focuses on the relationship between inputs and outputs without needing to know internal L1 mechanisms | Methodology | 0.92  
* Redundancy in systems ensures that if one component fails, the L1 goal can still be achieved via alternative L0 paths | Engineering | 0.95

## **Self-Organization and Meta-Cybernetics**

* Autopoiesis describes a system capable of reproducing and maintaining itself, a biological L1 rule for L0 life | System Theory | 0.93  
* Second-Order Cybernetics is the cybernetics of observing systems, where the observer is part of the system (User Modeling) | Epistemology | 0.91  
* Homeostasis in cybernetics is the L1 mathematical abstraction of biological steady-states | Biology Bridges | 0.96  
* Entropy in cybernetic systems represents the loss of control or organization over time | Information Theory | 0.95
