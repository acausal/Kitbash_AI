# **Game Theory**

## **Games and Strategies**

* A Game is an L1 mathematical model of strategic interaction between two or more rational agents | Foundations | 0.97  
* Players are the L0/L4 agents involved in the game, each seeking to maximize their L1 payoff | Agents | 0.96  
* A Strategy is a complete L1 plan of action for a player in a game | Logic | 0.95  
* Payoffs are the L0 outcomes representing the benefit or cost to a player for a given set of actions | Economics | 0.94  
* Zero-sum games are interactions where one player's gain is exactly equal to another's loss | Systems | 0.95

## **Equilibria and Dilemmas**

* Nash Equilibrium is an L1 stable state where no player can benefit by changing their strategy if others stay the same | Stability | 0.97  
* The Prisoner's Dilemma is an L3 heuristic showing why two rational agents might not cooperate even if it's in their best interest | Ethics | 0.95  
* Dominant Strategy is an L1 strategy that is best for a player regardless of what other players do | Logic | 0.96  
* Pareto Efficiency is an L1 state where no individual can be made better off without making someone else worse off | Economics | 0.94

## **Game Theory Meta-Context**

* Evolutionary Game Theory applies L1 game models to L0 biological populations and strategies | Biology | 0.93  
* Cooperation in game theory acts as a "Damping" mechanism against the L1 noise of conflict | Cybernetics | 0.94  
* Iterated games (repeating the same game) allow for the emergence of L1 trust and L2 narrative history | Sociology | 0.92  
* Common Knowledge is the L1 state where everyone knows X, and everyone knows that everyone knows X | Epistemology | 0.95
